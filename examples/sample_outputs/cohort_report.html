Xon aperiodic pipeline — cohort report

Recordings processed: 20. This report answers the study's core questions: can the Xon headset recover the aperiodic exponent accurately, is it reliable across a person's repeat sessions, does it survive a noisy condition, and how few minutes of clean data are needed.

Key takeaways

  • Test-retest reliability for movie is ICC(2,1) = 0.9814 (excellent reliability), from 5 participants.
  • Test-retest reliability for rest is ICC(2,1) = 0.9886 (excellent reliability), from 5 participants.
  • movie vs rest: exponent differs by -0.0267 on average (n=10 pairs, p=0.0575).
  • How few minutes are enough: internal-consistency (split-half) reliability reached 0.9 by 1.0 min; between-session reliability reached 'good' (ICC 0.75) by 1.0 min of clean data — direct evidence for the 'a few minutes instead of 8 hours' premise.
  • Across all recordings the mean fit r^2 was 0.9925 (higher = the 1/f slope genuinely fits the spectrum).

5. How few minutes are enough? (reliability vs recording length)

We estimate the exponent using increasing amounts of clean data and ask how reliable it becomes. Two standard measures: split-half internal consistency (odd vs even epochs, Spearman-Brown corrected; target ≥ 0.9) and between-session test-retest ICC (session 1 vs session 2; target ≥ 0.75 = "good"). The shortest recording length that reaches each target is the evidence-based answer to how few minutes are needed.
